Documentation

My inspiration for this experiment drew from pieces such as the following: Creative Coding ° Tusi Couple by dudenas and M_6_1_03 by Benedikt Groß, Hartmut Bohnacker, Julia Laub, Claudius Lazzeroni with contributions by Joey Lee and Niels Poldervaart.

I liked the look of the tree-like structure in patterns like binary search trees or in nature itself, so I wanted to try to emulate these characteristics in my experiment. I chose to start with the artwork “M_6_1_03” to base my experiment on.

Documentation

To get the “tree” structure that I was looking for, I disabled the “physics” components of the graph so that the points wouldn’t influence each other. I then restructured the generation of the points such that they branch outward from the bottom of the screen over time, with higher points having a wider width range to spawn in. I also changed the color scheme to a black-and-white aesthetic to highlight the branches.

Documentation

After playing around with the above code, I then progressed to developing my “tree” but ran into a surprising bug that caused my tree to transform into a rotating pentagon due to the incorrect math I used for generating the tree. Intrigued, I decided to change my idea from creating a tree into the generation of the illusion of three-dimensional shapes.

After some tweaking and fine-tuning, I came to a satisfying result with the randomly-colored “germs” and their varied sizes and movements, with the user being able to create more by clicking the screen with the mouse. I decided to call the work “Vector Germs” as the shapes were created entirely from vectors and they look somewhat like cartoon-styled germs.

    I really like the visual appeal of the experiment, from the way the geometric germs pulse to the way they move around in the black void.

  • Lows

    The binary tree that I originally intended to create didn’t come to fruition because I couldn’t figure out a proper way to implement it, but in its place I managed to create something more captivating and intriguing.
